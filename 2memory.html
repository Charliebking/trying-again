Charlie King

Memory Playground

Memory Playground is a duo exhibition featuring work by Charlie King and Maddie DeVoll that focuses on a variety of feelings about growing up. It is hard to grow past the trauma from childhood and allow yourself to heal, especially when a parent is in denial that you could be traumatized from their actions. While both artistsâ€™ childhood experiences are unique, they came together to create work that allows them to heal their inner child and shine light on how hard it can be to heal childhood trauma.

My photography and artwork explore the ideas of my experience growing up with a narcissistic mother in an abusive home. It has been hard to come to terms with my childhood and trying to allow myself to heal. The complexities of growing up in the south in a conservative home, identifying as queer, with unsupportive parents also influences how my childhood was impacted. My practice has allowed me to heal and connect with other about their experiences growing up. Collectively my work shows growing up and leaving behind everything you thought you could escape and finding yourself.
